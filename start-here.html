This page is for readers encountering Rule-Based Biology for the first time. It explains what this project is, why it exists, and how to navigate it.

What This Site Is

Rule-Based Biology presents a unified framework for understanding life using governing principles rather than descriptive accumulation of data.

It is not a theory, a hypothesis, or a speculative model. It is a reconstruction of biology based on rules that already exist in experimental data.

Why This Site Exists

Modern biology has produced enormous amounts of data, yet fundamental questions remain unresolved: disease mechanisms, regulation, energy usage, and biological decision-making.

The problem is not lack of data — it is lack of governing rules.

This site exists to show that once rules are identified, biological systems become coherent, predictable, and understandable.

How to Use This Site

  • If you are new, begin with Why Biology Failed.
  • To understand the foundation, read Why Rules.
  • To see the framework itself, go to Rule-Based Biology.
  • For implications, explore Applications.
  • If you are a student or researcher, see Students & Careers.

What This Is Not

  • Not an opinion site
  • Not speculative biology
  • Not an alternative medicine platform
  • Not a critique without solutions

The content here is based on internal consistency, logical necessity, and convergence of experimental evidence.

How to Read This Site

Read slowly. Follow the logic. Do not assume prior biological training is required.

The goal is understanding — not memorization.

When rules are known, complexity disappears.
