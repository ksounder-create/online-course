What Is Rule-Based Biology?

Rule-Based Biology reconstructs biological systems by identifying the governing rules that define how molecules, cells, and processes behave.

It does not interpret data. It aligns data with defining rules.

The Discovery

The central achievement of this work is the discovery of governing rules in biology.

These rules were not hypothesized or assumed. They were identified through systematic analysis of experimental data accumulated over decades.

Once these rules were recognized, two consequences became unavoidable:

  • Much of modern biology collapses as a descriptive framework.
  • A coherent, rule-based biology emerges in its place.

The discovery of rules did not extend existing biology. It dismantled it — and simultaneously made a new, rational biology possible.

Why This Exists

Modern biology developed without a validated foundation. As a result, explanations became fragmented, conditional, and contradictory.

This project exists to correct that. It provides a validated central framework for understanding life.

What Makes This Different

Rule-Based Biology does not add another theory or model.

It reconstructs biology from governing rules and resolves contradictions instead of accommodating them.
